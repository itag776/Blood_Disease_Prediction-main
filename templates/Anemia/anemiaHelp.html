Anemia module

Understanding the anemia screening result

This page explains how the anemia module works and how to interpret the output responsibly.

Screening Parameters Guide

How the module works

  1. You enter sex at birth, hemoglobin, and three red-cell indices.
  2. The model compares the submitted pattern with hematology examples used during training.
  3. The app returns a result label, risk score, and follow-up guidance.

How to interpret the result

  • A higher-risk result means the pattern resembles anemia-related profiles in the model.
  • A lower-risk result means the submitted values are less aligned with those patterns.
  • The output should support, not replace, clinical interpretation.

What the result does not tell you

The model does not explain the cause of anemia or identify a full subtype on its own. Clinical context and additional testing still matter.

Best next step after a higher-risk result

Review the CBC carefully, confirm the values, and follow up with a clinician for formal interpretation and possible additional laboratory work.

Medical disclaimer

This module is an educational screening aid and should not be treated as a standalone diagnostic tool.
