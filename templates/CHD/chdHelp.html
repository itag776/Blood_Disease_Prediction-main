Understanding the CHD screening result

This page explains what the coronary heart disease module measures and how to interpret the result responsibly.

Screening Parameters Guide

How the module works

  1. You enter demographic, lifestyle, medical-history, blood pressure, and laboratory data.
  2. The model evaluates how closely that profile aligns with patterns linked to future coronary heart disease risk.
  3. The app returns a result label, risk score, and a qualitative risk level.

How to interpret the result

  • A lower-risk result suggests the submitted profile is less aligned with higher CHD risk patterns in the model.
  • A higher-risk result suggests stronger alignment with those patterns.
  • The output is best used as a preventive screening signal, not a definitive cardiovascular diagnosis.

Important risk drivers

Smoking, hypertension, diabetes, cholesterol, age, and prior cardiovascular history meaningfully influence the result.

Best next step after a higher-risk result

Confirm the submitted data and discuss the result with a clinician who can review formal cardiovascular risk assessment options.

Medical disclaimer

This module supports early awareness and prevention conversations only. It does not replace medical diagnosis or emergency care.
